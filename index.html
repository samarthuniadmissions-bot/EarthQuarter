The idea

Most energy waste is quiet.

Earthquarter began with a simple observation: electricity is often wasted through small unnoticed habits, not through bad intentions. A light left on. A fan running in an empty room. A charger plugged in long after it is needed.

Everyday practice

Small actions become shared habits.

Families, schools, and students can make energy awareness visible by choosing one regular moment every week to pause, check, and switch off non-essential electricity.

Why it matters

Saving electricity also saves hidden resources.

Lower energy demand

Switching off unused appliances reduces the load created by everyday waste.

Water conservation

Using less electricity can reduce pressure on water used in parts of energy production and cooling.

Reduced emissions

Lower consumption means fewer avoidable emissions when electricity comes from carbon-heavy sources.

15-minute facts

See what switching off lights can save.

Electricity saved depends on the wattage of the lights and how many are switched off. Earthquarter uses the standard calculation: watts multiplied by time, divided by 1000.

Formula: lights x watts x 0.25 hours / 1000 = kWh saved per 15-minute switch-off.

10 LED lights using 9 watts each save about 0.023 kWh every Earthquarter.

How was this idea created?

Earthquarter began with one student's simple observation: electricity is often wasted not because people do not care, but because daily routines move too fast for anyone to notice what has been left on.

At home and at school, the same pattern kept appearing - lights in empty rooms, fans running after everyone had left, and chargers still plugged in long after they were needed. That small but repeated waste inspired a bigger question: what if one short habit each week could help people pause, notice, and act together?

That question became Earthquarter. The idea was to create a 15-minute weekly switch-off that feels easy to begin, meaningful to repeat, and powerful enough to spread from students to families, classrooms, and communities.

Vision:

Build a culture where young people lead practical climate action through awareness, consistency, and shared responsibility.
